The "Gigabyte Library" Problem

And Why I Built Beatr

If you're a music producer, you know the pain. You open a project from two years ago, and immediately get hit with "Missing Files: Kick_Deep_04.wav". Or you want to sketch out a quick idea, but you have to wait for your DAW to load gigabytes of sample libraries first.

Beatr is the answer to sample bloat. It's a synthesizer-first drum machine.

I built this using the Web Audio API to prove that you can get professional-sounding drum transients directly from the browser. Using oscillators, noise buffers, and precise envelopes, Beatr sculpts sound in real-time.

Is it free?

Yes. Beatr is free, open-source, and has no hidden subscriptions.

Whether you're sketching a new lo-fi track, practicing your finger drumming, or just need a metronome that doesn't suck, Beatr is ready when you are.
